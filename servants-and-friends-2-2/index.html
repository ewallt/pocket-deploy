When God Seems Severe

Severity can be the face of love when truth is the only cure.

God’s apparent harshness often conceals His deepest mercy. Maxwell explains that divine severity appears only when gentler means have failed. Like a surgeon who cuts to save, God intervenes decisively to preserve life and freedom for the many.

In Scripture, each crisis was moral before it was physical. The Flood, the plagues, and Israel’s captivities were moments when humanity’s self-destruction demanded interruption. God’s “wrath” is the steady withdrawal of protection — allowing sin to reap its fruit.

Jesus bore that same withdrawal on the cross, crying, “Why have You forsaken Me?” In that darkness, the severity of sin met the endurance of love. The scene reveals that God’s justice is His faithfulness to truth, not vengeance against His enemies.

When God seems severe, He is facing the cost of staying faithful to love in a world bent on ruin. The pain is His, not ours. Understanding this turns judgment stories into testimonies of divine patience.

Fear fades when we see the tears behind the thunder. Severity becomes compassion misunderstood.

Sources: *Servants or Friends?*, Ch. 7 “When God Seems Severe.”
