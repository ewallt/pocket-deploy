The Scientific Revolution

Five concise readers tracing how observation, mathematics, and faith in reason transformed humanity’s view of the cosmos.

Part 1 — The Turning of the Heavens

Copernicus challenges Ptolemy’s Earth-centered cosmos and quietly begins a revolution in thought.

Part 2 — Galileo’s Telescope and Trial

Observation meets authority as Galileo’s discoveries of moons and motion redefine evidence itself.

Part 3 — Kepler’s Laws and the Music of the Spheres

From circles to ellipses—how harmony became mathematics and the heavens began to sing in numbers.

Part 4 — Newton’s Principia

The synthesis of motion and gravity: the equations that unified Earth and sky.

Part 5 — The Enlightenment Legacy

From natural philosophy to modern science—reason, experiment, and the birth of modernity.
