      Why Networking Matters in SLC

      A strong network surfaces roles that never hit public boards—especially in culture-driven markets like Salt Lake City. The right rooms compress your search time and connect you with decision-makers who value operational excellence, integrity, and leadership.

      Industry Groups & Professional Associations

      Utah Association of CPAs (UACPA)

      Reliable in-person touchpoints through CPE events, golf tournaments, and ceremonies like the CPA Inauguration & Awards Banquet—ideal for accounting, audit, and controllership circles.

      How to engage
      • Arrive early to CPE sessions; sit near the front and ask one thoughtful question tied to close, controls, or analytics.
      • Follow up with speakers and panelists the same day via LinkedIn with a specific takeaway.

      CFO Leadership Council — SLC Chapter

      Target-rich for senior finance. Upcoming sessions include Economic Uncertainty: A CFO’s Survival Guide (Sept 24, 2025) and Networking & Cocktails (Oct 23, 2025 & Feb 25, 2026).

      Make it productive
      • Introduce yourself with a 15-second pitch emphasizing finance operations leadership, audit readiness, and process optimization.
      • Book 2–3 coffees from each mixer; confirm dates before leaving the room.

      Domain-Specific Events

      Fintech Xchange (Jan 23–24, 2025 @ Hyatt Regency SLC)

      Premier fintech forum joining technology, regulation, and academia—high signal for FP&A, RevRec (ASC 606), pricing, and SaaS metrics roles.

      Higher Education Financial Wellness Summit (July 13–16, 2025 @ University of Utah)

      Draws finance professionals focused on financial wellness and policy—excellent for university, non-profit, and grants/budgeting tracks.

      FEI Utah Chapter Events (Financial Executives International)

      Programs like Decode the Bottom Line: What DRIVES Financial Decision-Makers (Oct 22, 2025) convene senior leaders for thought leadership and networking.

      Event game plan (repeatable)
      1. Preview attendee list or speakers; pick 5 targets and 2 questions per target.
      2. Capture one actionable insight per session; reference it in follow-ups within 24 hours.
      3. Log outcomes (intros, leads, next steps) and schedule a weekly review.

      Private & Executive Networking

      Alta Club (Downtown SLC)

      Elite private club where social and business overlap among local decision-makers; membership or referrals can subtly open senior-level doors.

      100 Women in Finance — SLC Committee

      Programming (e.g., peer engagement lunches) creates inclusive crossover opportunities and access to leadership networks across asset classes.

      Discreet outreach tips
      • Ask existing contacts for a warm introduction rather than cold outreach.
      • Frame meetings around shared interests (automation, forecasting accuracy, audit readiness) instead of job seeking.

      Tactical Networking Strategies

      Where to Engage

      • Blend formal conferences (Fintech Xchange, FEI, UACPA) with informal mixers (CFO cocktails, Alta Club gatherings) to diversify connections.
      • Lead with a succinct pitch centered on finance ops leadership, audit, and process improvement.

      Conversation Starters

      • “How are you adapting close, forecast, and controls post-pandemic and with hybrid teams?”
      • “Which automation wins mattered most for your CFO org in the last 12 months?”

      Follow-Up Best Practices

      • Message within 24 hours with a specific takeaway or appreciation.
      • Share a relevant article/template and invite a short follow-up chat to exchange approaches.

      How This Helps You

      Strategic networking positions you as a peer and problem-solver in SLC’s finance community—not just a candidate. It lifts access to roles that combine responsibility, respect, and impact, aligning with your next-step goals.
