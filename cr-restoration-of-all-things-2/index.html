The Final Healing

*Evil ends not by force but by truth that makes deception impossible.*

The end of evil will not come through annihilating enemies but by removing the last falsehood. When truth is fully known, rebellion has no oxygen left to breathe. Those who cling to darkness face its natural outcome—the separation they insisted upon. Love’s justice is allowing freedom to finish its course.

The fire Scripture describes is not vindictive but revealing. It consumes lies and purifies love. God Himself is that consuming fire—the warmth that gives life or, resisted, becomes unbearable to the will opposed to it.

In the final reckoning, none are surprised. Every mind sees clearly what it has chosen. The lost recognize God’s goodness and bow, not coerced but convinced. The universe witnesses the harmony between mercy and truth.

Thus, destruction is not imposed violence but the final consequence of unreconciled distrust. Heaven does not rejoice in loss; it grieves even as it heals. The goal of judgment is closure without cruelty—an ending worthy of a God who never stopped loving.

When the smoke clears, only light remains. The tears of God will be the last tears shed in the universe.

Sources: Revelation 20:11–15; Hebrews 12:29; Philippians 2:10–11; Behold Your God (end of evil and divine justice).
