Part 3 — The Serpent’s Lie

“God is holding out on you.” The subtle reframe that turns gifts into suspicion and trust into calculation.

Recasting Motive: The lie doesn’t deny God—it maligns His intentions, making obedience seem servile and autonomy noble.

Rewriting Reality: Good boundaries are sold as bondage; consequences become threats; warnings sound like control.

Fruit of Suspicion: Hiding, blame, and fear appear—relational death before biological death.

Antidote: Only a clearer revelation of God’s heart can break the spell—argument alone cannot heal distrust.
