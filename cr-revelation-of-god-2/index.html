The Cross as the Key to All Things

*At Calvary, every question about God finds its answer.*

The universe’s deepest mystery is not how it was made but why it was redeemed. At the cross, infinite power consented to apparent defeat. What looked like failure was the unveiling of reality itself: love stronger than violence, truth enduring betrayal, life overcoming death by surrender.

All doctrines find coherence here. The justice of God is no longer vengeance but faithfulness to love. The mercy of God is not indulgence but restoration. The law, far from cold decree, becomes the description of how love behaves when nailed to wood.

Calvary redefines victory. The throne of the universe is shaped like a cross. Dominion is exercised through service; creation is sustained by compassion. The universe stands secure not because God cannot be challenged, but because His character cannot be falsified.

For angels and humans alike, this moment interprets every other. The risk of freedom, the patience of judgment, the silence of unanswered prayers—all are illuminated in that single light. What seemed chaos becomes coherence when viewed through crucified love.

In beholding the Crucified, heaven and earth learn the same lesson: that the One who suffers most loves most, and that such love alone has the right to reign forever.

Sources: 1 Corinthians 1:18–25; Romans 3:25–26; Behold Your God (ch. 2–3).
