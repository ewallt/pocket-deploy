      Thriving Local Economy

      Salt Lake City is one of the country’s strongest-performing metros, with a resilient labor market and steady tech expansion. It’s cementing its status as a Western hub where finance and tech intersect—drawing talent with competitive wages and quality of life.

      Active Demand for Finance Professionals

      Hundreds of finance roles are typically open at any time—ranging from Financial Analyst to Director of Finance—across healthcare, fintech, energy, real estate, education, and professional services.

      • Representative employers: University of Utah, Goldman Sachs (large ops/middle-office hub), Dyno Nobel, Comtel Group, and other regional leaders.
      • Common titles: FP&A Analyst/Manager, Accounting Manager, Controller, Finance Director, Revenue/Cost Analyst, Audit & Assurance, Treasury/Capital Markets, Operations Finance.
      Where roles tend to cluster
      • Enterprise & shared services: consolidation, close, reporting, SOX, audit support.
      • Tech/fintech: unit economics, SaaS metrics, RevRec (ASC 606), pricing, BI tooling.
      • Energy/industrial: plant/operations finance, cost, inventory, capex, hedge/accounting.
      • Healthcare & ed: budgeting, grants, reimbursement analytics, compliance.

      Compensation Snapshot

      Local pay is competitive for the region and aligns with mid-to-senior expectations:

      • Financial Analyst (avg.): ~$90,200 base; ~$97,000 total comp.
      • Analyst (broader category): total pay ~$97,000 on average (range ~$76K–$126K); higher in energy, real estate, and tech.
      • Industry medians (illustrative): Manufacturing ~$88.7K; Financial Services ~$86.2K; Consulting ~$81.6K.

      Note: Figures are directional for 2025 planning and can vary by company size, scope, and credentials (CPA, MBA, Big 4, systems depth).

      Flexibility and Work Arrangements

      Hybrid work continues to expand across finance—especially in tech-driven and professional services firms—while many roles remain in-person for operational continuity. Roughly half of employers now offer some hybrid option in applicable functions.

      • Hybrid hotspots: FP&A, corporate development, BI/analytics, audit, controllership in larger orgs.
      • Primarily on-site: plant/operations finance, inventory/cost accounting, certain treasury/ops roles.

      What This Means for You

      • Right roles are active: Finance operations leadership, analysis, audit, budgeting, consolidation—these map well to SLC demand.
      • Skills match: Process improvement, close/consolidation, planning/budgeting, and audit experience align with mid-to-senior roles at scale.
      • Pay fit: SLC compensation ranges are consistent with your targets; sector choice (energy, real estate, tech) can lift upside.
      • Flexibility: Growing hybrid options support your preferred work style while preserving on-site leadership presence when needed.
      Quick next steps (30–60 days)
      1. Target list: 12–15 employers split across healthcare, tech/fintech, energy/industrial, and higher-ed.
      2. Resume focus: 3–5 bullets per role quantifying process improvements (close days ↓, forecast accuracy ↑, audit findings ↓).
      3. Tooling: Highlight Excel/Sheets mastery, ERP experience (NetSuite/SAP/Oracle), and BI (Power BI/Tableau/Looker).
      4. Outreach: Warm intros to hiring managers/controllers; reverse-shadow job posts to mirror language in your materials.
      5. Interview prep: SLC-relevant case prompts (cash flow bridge, price/volume/mix, variance walk, 3-statement linkage).
