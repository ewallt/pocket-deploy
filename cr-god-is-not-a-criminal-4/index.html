The Misjudged God

*We often mistake divine accommodation for endorsement.*

Like the “hunter father” who instructs a stubborn child to minimize harm, God sometimes appears to participate in human systems He does not approve. Outsiders overhear the accommodation and assume approval. But love’s involvement is saving presence, not complicity. Without it, we would be left to ourselves.

Israel demanded kings, wars, and rituals that mirrored the nations. God stayed with them, guiding within the limits they chose. Prophets later clarified that these concessions revealed our hearts more than His. He preferred mercy to sacrifice, justice to spectacle, knowledge of God to victory parades.

This misreading persists whenever we confuse permission with prescription. Divine patience gets recast as delay, warning as threat, consequence as cruelty. The cross exposes our error: God enters our violence to rescue, not to validate it.

To behold God truly is to re-read the hard texts through the character of Christ. Where the surface seems harsh, the context tells another story—love staying engaged where rejection would be easier. The Judge turns out to be the Saviour at work.

Misjudgment fades as we recognize the pattern: God’s methods never change; only our perceptions do. He is not a criminal; He is the Friend who refuses to leave.

Sources: 1 Samuel 8; Hosea 6:6; Matthew 9:13; Behold Your God (accommodation vs. approval).
