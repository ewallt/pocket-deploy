      Leverage the Referral Culture

      Many Utah employers prioritize referrals. Tell your network exactly what you’re targeting (titles, sectors, company list) and ask for warm introductions to hiring managers or controllers.

      • Circulate a tight “target roles & companies” one-pager to close friends, prior managers, and community contacts.
      • Offer a short blurb they can paste into an intro email or Slack—make helping you effortless.
      • Reciprocate: share leads back and connect others to reinforce the norms of SLC’s referral culture.
      Intro message template (copy/paste)

      Subject: Quick intro? Finance leader open to hybrid roles

      Hi [Name] — I’m exploring senior finance roles in SLC (FP&A/Controllership/Operations Finance). I’ve led close acceleration, forecast accuracy lifts, and audit readiness. If [Hiring Manager/Dept] is open to a brief chat, happy to share specifics and relevant wins. Thanks for any steer or intro you’re comfortable making.

      Use LinkedIn Strategically

      Follow target companies, comment on local finance posts, and send personalized connection requests that reference shared interests or mutuals.

      • Post weekly: a short insight on close/forecast improvements or finance automation wins (2–4 sentences + one takeaway).
      • Comment daily on 3–5 local posts with a concrete observation; avoid generic praise.
      • Build a focused SLC list (finance leaders, controllers, BI leads, recruiters) and work it consistently.
      Connection request templates
      • Peer: “Enjoyed your note on forecast cadence at [Company]. I led a similar shift (accuracy 82%→93%). Would love to connect with fellow SLC finance folks.”
      • Hiring manager: “I’ve run close, planning, and audit readiness in hybrid teams. Following [Company] and your ops posts—mind a connection?”
      • Recruiter: “Senior finance leader (FP&A/controllership). Open to hybrid SLC roles. Happy to share metrics and team outcomes—open to connect?”
      10-minute daily routine
      1. Scan your SLC list; comment meaningfully on 3 posts.
      2. Send 2 targeted requests with a specific reference.
      3. DM 1 prior contact with an article/template and ask a question.

      Monitor Specialized Job Boards

      Hidden roles often appear first on niche or association boards before (or instead of) the big aggregators.

      • BuiltIn Utah: tech/fintech FP&A, RevRec, BI/analytics, and strategy roles.
      • Utah Nonprofits Association: finance leadership in nonprofit/education sectors.
      • Association boards: UACPA Careers, FEI Career Center for senior finance openings.
      • Company pages first: Zions Bancorp, University of Utah, healthcare systems, energy/industrial firms.
      Saved search strings (easy copy)
      • site:builtin.com "Utah" (FP&A OR "finance manager" OR controller)
      • site:uacpa.org (controller OR "accounting manager" OR audit) "Salt Lake"
      • "finance" (hybrid OR flexible) ("Salt Lake City" OR Utah) (forecast OR variance OR close)

      Engage with Recruiters

      Identify SLC-based recruiters who focus on finance/accounting. Send a tailored resume and be explicit about scope, comp band, and hybrid availability.

      • Lead with metrics (close days ↓, forecast accuracy ↑, audit findings ↓, automation adoption ↑).
      • Clarify preferences: sectors (tech/fintech, healthcare, education, energy), team size, and systems (NetSuite/SAP/Oracle, BI).
      • Request 15 minutes to align on current pipelines and timing.
      Outbound note to a recruiter

      Hi [Name], I lead finance operations (close, planning, audit readiness) and recently improved forecast accuracy from 82%→93% while shortening close by 3 days. I’m targeting hybrid SLC roles (manager/director/controllership). Happy to send a one-page impact summary—open to a quick alignment chat?

      What to ask recruiters
      • “Which clients are leaning hybrid for finance leadership?”
      • “Where do my outcomes map to urgent gaps you’re filling now?”
      • “What 2–3 phrasing tweaks would move me to the top of your shortlist?”

      Attend Industry Events

      Use conferences, association meetings, and trainings to hear about openings before they post—and to secure warm intros on the spot.

      • Arrive early, sit near speakers, and ask one specific question tied to your expertise.
      • Queue brief coffees: “Would you be open to a 15-minute chat this week on how you approached X?”
      • Leave each event with 2 scheduled follow-ups, not just business cards.
      Conversation openers & follow-ups
      • Onsite opener: “Curious how your team balances hybrid rhythms with close deadlines—what’s actually working?”
      • Next-day note: “Appreciated your take on variance drivers at [Event]. Here’s a one-pager on my FP&A process changes—open to a quick exchange of approaches?”
