The Joy of Friendship

Friendship with God is the atmosphere of eternity and the medicine of now.

Maxwell loved to say that God wants friends, not slaves. Friendship is mutual trust between unequals, made possible because the Greater has stooped to share our life in Jesus. The result is joy — not the thrill of escape, but the delight of communion.

In friendship, obedience is agreement. We do what God asks because we see what He sees. The law reads like a description of life with Someone we admire.

Joy flourishes where fear has faded. Prayer becomes relaxed honesty; confession feels safe; hope stops whispering and starts singing. This is the emotional climate of the new creation.

Friendship also energizes mission. People share good news when they love the One they’re talking about. Atonement thus produces witnesses whose testimony is joy.

The universe will be secure because its citizens enjoy God. Friendship is hard to betray when it is this beautiful. Love has made loyalty sweet.

To be at one with God is to be at home. Joy is the sound of homecoming.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations (Pine Knoll PDF), entries on “Friends of God,” “Joy,” “Law.”
