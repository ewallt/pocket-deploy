Grace That Liberates: A Jesus-Centered Gospel

How the Good News Reframes God, Sin, Faith, and the Church

Core Thesis

The gospel is not a sin-management plan or a religious ladder; it is the announcement that God is exactly like Jesus and that His relentless grace is already reaching for every person (John 14:9; Luke 19:10). In Jesus we see that God condemns sin because it destroys us, yet He refuses to condemn sinners‚ÄîHe forgives, restores, and invites us to follow Him into real life (John 8:11; Luke 15). Faith is trusting this good news enough to walk in it; discipleship is learning to love like Jesus in everyday life (Galatians 5:6; Ephesians 2:8‚Äì10). The church exists not to guard boundaries but to embody hospitality, reconciliation, and practical mercy that mirror the Father‚Äôs heart (2 Corinthians 5:18‚Äì20; Matthew 9:13).

Jeremy Myers: Redeeming God from Religion

How a burned-out pastor learned to read the Bible through Jesus' eyes and discovered a grace that liberates rather than a religion that controls.

Contemporary evangelical writer & podcaster
Founder of RedeemingGod.com
Key themes: God looks like Jesus, grace before change, relational faith, open table

Jeremy Myers grew up in evangelical Christianity and felt called to pastoral ministry at a young age. He attended seminary, planted churches, and preached the gospel with energy and conviction. But over time he began to notice something troubling: the gospel he was preaching seemed to produce more fear than freedom, more insiders and outsiders than reconciliation, more religion than relationship. People were trying harder, feeling guiltier, and finding less rest. Myers himself felt the weight of it‚Äîthe endless striving to be good enough, to get the doctrine right, to keep people in line.

The turning point came when Myers began to ask a simple but radical question: What if God is exactly like Jesus? Not similar to Jesus in some ways while being harsher or more distant in others, but exactly like Jesus‚Äîall the way down. If Jesus welcomed sinners, ate with tax collectors, forgave enemies, and refused to condemn the woman caught in adultery, then that is what God is like. Always. Everywhere. No exceptions. This meant that much of what Myers had been taught about God‚Äîthe wrath that needed appeasement, the holiness that required distance, the judgment that waited to pounce‚Äîhad to be rethought from the ground up.

Myers left traditional pastoral ministry and began writing and teaching through his website RedeemingGod.com, where he invites burned-out and disillusioned Christians to "break free from religion" and rediscover Jesus. His books and podcasts circle around a handful of core convictions: God's grace comes before our change, not after it. Repentance is a response to experienced kindness, not a prerequisite for love. Faith is relational trust, not intellectual assent to propositions. And the church is called to practice the wide hospitality Jesus modeled‚Äîtable fellowship with the "wrong" people‚Äîrather than policing boundaries.

Grace as Liberation, Not License

Myers is quick to clarify that grace is not permission to harm. Grace liberates us from the slavery of sin and fear precisely because it heals and transforms. But transformation comes from the inside out, as we behold the goodness of God in Christ and find our hearts changed. This is why Myers critiques traditional penal-substitution models of atonement that portray the Father venting wrath on the Son. For him, such pictures split the Trinity and obscure the truth that God was in Christ reconciling the world. The cross reveals that God absorbs our violence, forgives our betrayal, and invites us back‚Äîwithout conditions, without payback, without waiting for us to clean up first.

This vision led Myers to reread the difficult texts of Scripture through the Jesus lens. Passages that seem to portray God as violent, capricious, or conditional must be held up against the cross and reinterpreted. Jesus is not one voice among many; He is the final word, the full revelation, the lens that brings everything else into focus. And that lens reveals a God whose love is relentless, whose mercy is first and last, and whose kingdom is characterized by generosity, forgiveness, and peace.
