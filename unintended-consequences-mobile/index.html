Osborne Reef (Florida)

South Florida wanted an artificial reef to boost marine habitat and tourism.

Sink ~2 million discarded car tires offshore—cheap, durable, easy to stack.

Storms snapped the ties; tires roamed the seafloor, sandblasting natural reefs and entangling wildlife. Cleanup divers are still chasing the “permanent” fix.

Corn Ethanol Subsidies

Reduce oil dependence and help the climate, preferably with a patriotic crop.

Mandate/bless ethanol blending and subsidize corn-based biofuel production.

Land and feed prices jumped, marginal acres got plowed, and the net climate benefit proved…diet-soda sized once you count fertilizer and fuel.

ACA Employer Coverage Quirk

Keep workers insured without dumping them onto the individual market.

Craft employer-mandate rules and plan definitions to prevent coverage games.

Some firms trimmed hours or benefits to thread the letter of the law—draw a box and payroll learns origami.

Rent Control

Keep housing affordable for tenants in expensive cities.

Cap allowable rent increases and limit evictions to protect existing renters.

Landlords convert units to condos or withdraw them from rental markets; new construction slows. The lucky few keep cheap flats—everyone else finds fewer flats to rent at all.

Minimum Wage Increases

Raise incomes for low-wage workers and reduce inequality.

Mandate higher hourly pay.

Some firms cut hours, automate tasks, or hire fewer entry-level workers. A few workers earn more; others earn nothing. The policy gains headlines faster than experience.

Corporate Tax Hikes

Fund public services and ensure corporations “pay their fair share.”

Raise corporate income tax rates.

Investment shifts abroad, wages stagnate, and tax avoidance blooms—proving that capital, like water, prefers downhill gradients.

Nuisance Laws

Curb activities that threaten health or safety (noise, hazardous waste, criminal activity).

Enact “nuisance” ordinances that penalize repeat incidents with fines or eviction.

Victims (e.g., domestic violence survivors) can be labeled the nuisance and evicted, discouraging future calls for help and making neighborhoods less safe.

Jaywalking Laws

Reduce pedestrian crashes.

Ticket crossings outside marked crosswalks; raise fines to deter risky behavior.

Criminalizes normal responses to car-centric street design; imposes high fines on people who walk and can lead to extreme prosecutions when safe crossings are impractically far away.

Fines for Minor Infractions

Encourage compliance with local rules (traffic, parking, small code violations).

Use fines, fees, and court costs; suspend licenses or escalate penalties for nonpayment.

Costs snowball into debt, license loss, job loss, and even jail—effectively penalizing poverty rather than deterring harm.

South Asia’s Vulture Collapse

Farmers in South Asia needed affordable pain relief for working livestock.

Widespread veterinary use of diclofenac: effective, cheap, and great for sore bovines—so routine that residues were common in carcasses left for scavengers.

Vultures ate those carcasses, suffered kidney failure, and crashed by ~95%. With vultures gone, dog populations swelled around rotting carrion, driving more rabies risk.

Kingpin Decapitation Strategy

Drug-cartel violence in Latin America, with governments under pressure to show decisive progress.

“Decapitation” strategies: target kingpins for arrest or neutralisation, plus high-visibility crackdowns to disrupt trafficking routes. Looks tough, polls great.

Remove a boss and you invite splintering and turf wars. Evidence from Mexico links kingpin targeting to cartel fragmentation and sharply higher homicide rates—cut off the head, grow three.

School Cash Transfers (Morocco)

Morocco wanted to cut primary-school dropout and boost enrolment.

Scale up a conditional cash transfer (Tayssir), paying families if children enrol and attend. It worked: dropout fell by about 1.3 percentage points.

Supply didn’t scale with demand. Average class size rose by 3.6 students and test scores fell. Schooling went up; learning went down—at least until resources caught up.
