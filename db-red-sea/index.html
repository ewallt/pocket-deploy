Red Sea

Red Sea: Deliverance and Divine Intervention

A theological exploration of the Red Sea crossing, examining its significance as a foundational narrative of liberation, divine power, and God's covenant faithfulness in both biblical history and contemporary faith understanding.

The Exodus Experience

A journey through the defining narrative of Israel's liberation from Egypt, exploring the theological, historical, and spiritual dimensions of the Exodus story and its enduring impact on faith, identity, and understanding of God's redemptive work.

Typology in the Sanctuary and Exodus

An exploration of biblical typology connecting the sanctuary services and the Exodus experience, examining how these Old Testament patterns prefigure and illuminate New Testament spiritual realities and God's plan of redemption.

Jesus and the New Exodus

An examination of Jesus as the new Israel experiencing a new Exodus, exploring how the Gospel writers present Christ's ministry as a recapitulation and fulfillment of Israel's foundational deliverance narrative, establishing a new covenant and liberation from sin.

The Song of Miriam

A study of the Song of Miriam (also known as the Song of Moses) in Exodus 15, exploring this ancient hymn of victory at the Red Sea, its role in Israel's worship tradition, and the prophetic leadership of Miriam in celebrating God's deliverance.
