Part 1 — The Mystery of Rebellion

Freedom implies risk; trust makes freedom safe. How a perfect mind could choose distrust—and why God would allow it.

Freedom & Love: Coerced loyalty is contradiction. Love requires freedom; freedom creates the genuine possibility of “No.”

The First Doubt: The seed of suspicion—imagining God as self-protective—begins the moral fracture long before overt acts.

Why Permit It? Preventing evil by removing freedom destroys the very good creation was meant to enjoy. God chooses patient truth over force.

Trajectory of Mistrust: Distrust curves the self inward; isolation and violence follow as reality is recast around self-advantage.
