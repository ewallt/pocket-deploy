The Freedom of Love

Love cannot exist without freedom; the cross defends both forever.

Freedom is the condition of love, and love is the meaning of freedom. Maxwell stressed that God’s government is never maintained by fear or force but by truth believed in freedom.

The cross proved this principle to the universe. When rebellion challenged God’s character, He did not silence His critics by power but by self-exposure. He showed that freedom is so sacred He would die rather than violate it.

Such love creates security without control. Creatures remain free, yet they choose to trust the One who never abuses power. This is the only foundation for eternal peace.

Religion often distorts freedom into license or fear of losing it. Atonement reveals freedom as belonging — the glad independence of those who love God because they know Him.

When the cross is understood this way, obedience and freedom cease to compete. Both thrive in the light of truth and the presence of love.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations (Pine Knoll PDF), entries on “Freedom,” “Love,” “Government of God.”
