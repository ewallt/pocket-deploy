Love’s Fire

The glory that saves the trusting consumes only what cannot live with love.

Biblical fire-language often frightens because we imagine retaliatory flames. Maxwell reframes it as the unveiled presence of God—pure, healing, and honest. The same presence that warms the faithful scalds deception.

Love’s fire is not aimed but inherent. To reject the Source of life is to choose what cannot survive the light of truth. The end of sin is the end of falsehood in the blaze of reality.

At the cross this fire burned brightest, not in outward destruction but in suffering love. Jesus bore the heat of our estrangement so we could stand in that light without fear.

Final cleansing is therefore consistent with Calvary: God removes what harms because He is wholly for life. Nothing arbitrary enters His work.

For the friends of God, love’s fire is home. It clarifies, purifies, and secures a world where nothing unloving remains.

That is why judgment is good news—because it ends the last shadows love did not cast.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations, entries on “Fire,” “Presence,” “Glory.”
