Faith That Can Face God

Perfect love drives out fear — and perfect trust welcomes judgment.

To stand before God without terror is the climax of the gospel. Maxwell ends the first section by showing that genuine faith does not shrink from divine scrutiny. When we know God as Jesus revealed Him, judgment becomes reunion, not threat.

The prophets called people to “reason together” with God, not to cower. The final crisis will separate those who fear God’s presence from those who find safety in it. The same glory that consumes rebellion sustains life in the redeemed.

Faith that can face God is confidence in His fairness. It is the mature trust of friends who know they are understood. This faith is not presumption but relationship — the assurance that love never fails.

Servants obey because they must; friends obey because they agree. The transformation of fear into trust is the essence of salvation. Every doctrine, ritual, and command finds its purpose in restoring that friendship.

When friendship replaces fear, heaven begins now. The universe is secure not by power but by trust in the God who is love.

Sources: *Servants or Friends?*, Ch. 5 “Faith That Can Face God.”
