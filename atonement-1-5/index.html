Peace with God

Reconciliation produces the deep assurance that nothing stands between us and God.

The goal of atonement is peace — not a truce with an offended Sovereign, but the quiet confidence of friendship restored. Maxwell’s selections stress that this peace comes from knowing God as Jesus revealed Him, not from legal maneuvering.

Peace grows where truth is believed. When the cross persuades the heart that God is entirely for us, fear loses its rationale and guilt its sting. Faith becomes more than assent; it becomes trust that dares to be honest with God.

Such peace changes behavior without the pressure of surveillance. Gratitude replaces anxiety as the motive for obedience. The same love that reassures us also reshapes us.

In the cosmic dimension, peace means security for the universe. Everyone will know by experience that God’s way alone sustains life and freedom. Rebellion will be unthinkable because it has been seen through.

To live “at one” with God is to enjoy Him. Prayer becomes conversation; worship becomes delight. This is the life atonement makes possible now and forever.

Friends do not fear the day they meet. That is what it means to have peace with God.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations (Pine Knoll PDF), entries on “Peace,” “Reconciliation,” “Faith.”
