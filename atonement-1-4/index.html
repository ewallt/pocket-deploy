Truth as God’s Method

God governs by revelation and consent; coercion belongs to another kingdom.

From Genesis to Jesus, God’s strategy is consistent: He tells the truth and invites trust. Maxwell’s quotations circle this theme again and again. Evidence, patience, and freedom are the tools of divine government.

In the great controversy, lies cannot be expelled by force. They must be exposed. The cross unmasked the Adversary’s charges and displayed the cost of love’s refusal to retaliate. This disclosure secured the universe more than any display of raw power could.

Because God rules by truth, He also rules by freedom. He gives room to disagree, to question, even to rebel — and then answers those questions with further evidence. Such openness would be dangerous if God were not what Jesus revealed Him to be.

This method extends into judgment. The final assize is public, transparent, and persuasive. Every mind will see enough to choose freely, and the verdict will be the universe’s, not merely the Judge’s.

Religion often seeks shortcuts to certainty through control. God never does. He waits until the truth can be safely believed, because trust built on anything less would not endure.

Truth wins because love tells it and because love is what the truth reveals. That is why atonement heals.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations (Pine Knoll PDF), entries on “Truth,” “Revelation,” “Great Controversy.”
