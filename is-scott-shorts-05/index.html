Heaven: Where Does God Live?

From cosmic courtyard to living temples.

Big Idea (Snapshot)

▼

Scripture speaks of heaven as God’s dwelling place, yet also insists that no building and no stretch of sky can contain Him. The vast universe is like His courtyard, but His favorite home is more personal: the minds and hearts of creatures who freely welcome His presence. Heaven is not only “out there”; it is wherever God is known, trusted, and loved—where His Spirit finds a living temple.

Key Texts

▼

1 Kings 8:27 — Even the highest heaven cannot contain God.

Acts 17:24 — God does not dwell in temples made with hands.

Psalm 90:1 — “Lord, Thou hast been our dwelling place in all generations.”

1 Peter 2:5 — We are living stones built into a spiritual house.

Colossians 1:27 — “Christ in you, the hope of glory.”

What’s Gone Wrong (Distortion)

▼

On this planet, the “most holy place” of many minds has been invaded by lies about God’s character. Instead of a dwelling of praise and trust, the inner temple can become a marketplace of fear, self-protection, and self-worship. When intelligent beings shut out the God of life and love, they drift toward a kind of inner nothingness—alive biologically, but disconnected from the Source who gives meaning and joy.

What Jesus Shows Us

▼

At the cross, God begins a great restoration project. He confronts the lies that drove Him out of human hearts and wins us back by self-giving love. As we respond, Jesus cleanses the inner temple, sweeps away mistrust, and joins His thoughts to ours. Heaven and earth meet wherever His presence is welcomed within us, and our lives become small but real outposts of the coming new creation.

Heart Work & Reflection

▼

What picture of God lives in your inner “most holy place”? Is He distant, unpredictable, or harsh—or the Father Jesus reveals? Where have cynicism or disappointment pushed Him to the margins? Invite Christ to dwell in you again, to make your mind a place where His presence is welcomed and His love is trusted.

Gospel Thread

▼

The good news is not merely that we will someday go to heaven, but that the God of heaven has come near and desires to live in us. By His Spirit, He is building a worldwide temple of living stones, joined in worship and love. When Christ, who is our life, appears, those who have welcomed Him within will also appear with Him in glory.
