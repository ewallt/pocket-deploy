Behold Your God

*The everlasting gospel is the everlasting character of God—love revealed, trusted, and enjoyed forever.*

The journey ends where it began: with a call to behold. The good news is not merely a plan of rescue but the Person who rescues. In Christ we see the truth about God so clearly that worship becomes the most reasonable act of a grateful mind.

The cross remains the center of our sight. It is the grammar of the kingdom—the way love speaks in a fallen world and the way love will be remembered in a healed one. The resurrection guarantees that this love governs reality now and forever.

Discipleship in the age to come is simply deeper beholding. Learning never ends because love is infinite. We will read our histories in a new key, hearing mercy under every note and wisdom in every rest.

The final word over fear is joy. The Name once maligned is hallowed, the family reconciled, the universe at peace. Authority is trusted because it has been tested, and freedom is safe because it loves what is true.

So the invitation stands, as fresh in glory as it was in Galilee: behold your God. In His light we see light, and in His love we are finally home.

Sources: Isaiah 40:9; Revelation 14:6–7; 2 Corinthians 3:18; Behold Your God (beholding and transformation).
