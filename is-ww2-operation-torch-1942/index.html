Operation Torch (1942)

Allied Landings in North Africa and the Opening of a New Front

Core Summary

Launched on November 8, 1942, Operation Torch was the first major Allied amphibious operation of World War II, landing American and British forces in French Morocco and Algeria. The operation opened a second front against the Axis, coordinated with British advances from Egypt, and initiated the campaign that would drive Axis forces from North Africa by May 1943. Torch marked America's entry into ground combat in the European theater.
