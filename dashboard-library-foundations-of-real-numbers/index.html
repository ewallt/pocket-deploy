Foundations of Real Numbers — Library

This library presents a compact, mobile-first exploration of how the real numbers are constructed and why completeness matters. It emphasizes the logical pressure that drove the transition from rational arithmetic to a robust continuum, showing how definitions such as least upper bound, Cauchy convergence, and Dedekind cuts resolve historical paradoxes and stabilize analysis. Each article is standalone and self-contained.
