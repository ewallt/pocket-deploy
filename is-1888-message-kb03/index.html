1888, the Latter Rain, and the Loud Cry: Preparing for Christ's Coming

The gospel message that ripens a people for the harvest

The 1888 message of Christ's righteousness was intended to be the beginning of the latter rain and loud cry, initiating the final outpouring of the Holy Spirit needed to complete God's work before the Second Coming. Ellen White testified that this message was God's appointed means to ripen a people for the harvest, leading them to deeper repentance and heart conversion that would open the way for the Spirit to work more fully in and through the church. The reception or rejection of this message directly affects the timing and progress of the final work—resistance shut away the special power of the Holy Spirit that God longed to impart, delaying the fullness of the intended blessing. The gospel, rightly understood and centered on Christ's righteousness, is not peripheral but central to preparation for the Second Coming. True readiness comes not through intensified law-keeping or fear-based religion but through receiving Christ by faith, which produces love, obedience, and Christlike character as its fruit. This message demonstrates how Adventist distinctives find their meaning and power when rooted in the gospel rather than standing apart from it.

Key Scriptures

▼
Joel 2:23

The promise of early and latter rain connects agricultural blessing to spiritual outpouring; the 1888 message was understood as the beginning of the latter rain that ripens the harvest.

Joel 2:28-32

The outpouring of the Spirit in the last days; the 1888 emphasis on Christ's righteousness was meant to prepare hearts for this fuller empowerment and final witness.

Zechariah 10:1

"Ask for rain in the time of the latter rain"; receiving the 1888 message with faith was seen as asking for and receiving the promised Spirit baptism.

Revelation 14:6-12

The three angels' messages provide the content; 1888 was identified as presenting these messages "in verity," centered on Christ's righteousness rather than law-keeping.

Revelation 18:1

The angel whose glory fills the earth; Ellen White called the 1888 message "the beginning of the glory" of this angel, initiating worldwide illumination of God's character.

Matthew 13:30, 39

Harvest imagery depicting the end of the age; the gospel message ripens believers' characters, preparing them to stand at Christ's coming.

Revelation 22:11

Ripeness and fixity of character before Christ returns; the 1888 message was meant to hasten character development through faith in Christ's righteousness.

Matthew 24:14

The gospel preached to all the world, then the end comes; the loud cry beginning in 1888 was God's provision for completing this final witness.

2 Peter 3:9, 12

The Lord's patience and the hastening of His coming; the message's reception affects the timing, as God waits for His people to be ready.

Core Concepts

▼
Latter Rain as Intensified Work of the Spirit

The latter rain is not merely more power added to unchanged hearts, but the Spirit's fuller work in those who have received Christ by faith, enabling them to reflect His character and proclaim His righteousness with supernatural effectiveness.

Loud Cry as Gospel-Centered Proclamation

The loud cry is the three angels' messages amplified and illuminated by the revelation of Christ's righteousness, making Adventist truth compelling good news rather than fearful warnings.

Christ's Righteousness as the Glory that Lightens the Earth

The "glory" of Revelation 18 is not institutional success or prophetic accuracy but the manifestation of Christ's character through His people, drawing the world to see God's love and justice.

Ripening of Character and Harvest

Just as grain ripens under the latter rain, believers' characters mature under the gospel message—faith produces love, love produces obedience, and obedience reveals Christlikeness, preparing them for the harvest.

Conditional Element in the Timing of the End

While God's sovereignty is certain, the readiness of His people affects the timing—receiving the message hastens the coming, while resistance delays the fullness of blessing and prolongs the wait.

Integration of Adventist Distinctives with the Gospel

The Sabbath, sanctuary truth, and prophetic identity are not competing with the gospel but find their meaning within it—they are expressions of Christ's lordship and kingdom, not tests divorced from grace.

Preparation Through Gospel, Not Performance

True readiness for Christ's coming flows from faith in His finished work, not anxious striving to perfect oneself—the gospel produces what law-centered religion cannot: genuine love and transformed hearts.

Deep Repentance and Heart Conversion

The 1888 message was designed to lead believers beyond formal correctness to deep sorrow for sin and genuine union with Christ, opening the way for the Spirit's fuller indwelling.

How 1888 Relates to the Latter Rain

▼
Intended as the Beginning

Ellen White testified that the 1888 message represented the commencement of the latter rain, the front edge of the final outpouring that would empower the church to finish the work and ripen for the harvest.

Link Between Receiving and Outpouring

Receiving the message with the heart—not merely acknowledging it intellectually—was necessary for the deeper outpouring of the Spirit, as faith opens the channel for God's power.

Resistance Shut Away the Blessing

Opposition and unbelief prevented the intended fullness; Satan "shut away from God's people...the special power of the Holy Spirit that God longed to impart."

Early Rain as Preparation

Just as early rain softens the soil for planting, earlier gospel work prepared hearts; the latter rain was meant to mature and ripen what the early rain began, bringing the harvest to completion.

Ongoing Availability

Though resisted in 1888, the message remains heaven's provision—each generation is called to receive it, opening the way for the Spirit's work that has been offered but not yet fully realized.

Character Ripening Through the Spirit

The latter rain does not bypass character development but accelerates it through deeper surrender and fuller trust in Christ, producing fruit that glorifies God and stands the test of the final harvest.

How 1888 Relates to the Loud Cry

▼
Revelation of Christ's Righteousness as the Glory

The "glory" of the Revelation 18 angel is not organizational strength or prophetic knowledge but the manifestation of Christ's character, revealed supremely through the message of His righteousness received by faith.

Three Angels' Messages Become Good News

When centered in the gospel, Adventist proclamation transforms from warnings about beasts and compliance into joyful announcement of Christ's kingdom, the Creator's love, and salvation through faith alone.

Loud Cry Shaped by Gospel Centrality

The final worldwide witness gains its power not from doctrinal precision alone but from hearts aflame with love for Christ, proclaiming His righteousness as the only hope for a dying world.

Beginning, Not Completion

The 1888 message initiated the loud cry but did not exhaust it—resistance delayed the fullness, meaning each generation is called to take up the message and proclaim it with renewed power.

Light that Fills the Earth

Ellen White identified 1888 as "the beginning of the glory of the angel of Revelation 18, whose splendor will fill the whole earth"—the gospel of Christ's righteousness is the light that illuminates God's character globally.

Preparation for the Second Coming

▼
Faith Produces Love and Obedience

Gospel preparation creates believers who keep God's commandments not from fear or self-effort but from hearts transformed by grace, manifesting "Christ's righteousness...in obedience to all the commandments of God."

Character Ripening Through Union with Christ

Readiness for the Second Coming is not achieving sinless perfection through striving but growing into Christ's likeness through abiding in Him, as the branch bears fruit by remaining in the vine.

Deep Repentance and Heart Conversion

The 1888 message was designed to lead believers past superficial religion to genuine sorrow for sin and living union with Jesus, producing the depth of spirituality needed to stand in the last days.

Opening for the Spirit's Fuller Work

Receiving the gospel message with the heart removes barriers to the Holy Spirit's indwelling, allowing Him to work more fully in sanctifying, empowering, and preparing God's people for the harvest.

Different from Law-Centered Religion

Preparation through the gospel differs radically from fear-based striving—it replaces anxiety with assurance, performance with trust, and self-focus with Christ-focus, producing peace and fruitfulness.

Loving What God Loves, Hating What He Hates

Gospel transformation aligns the believer's affections with God's, creating hearts that naturally love righteousness and hate sin because they have seen Christ's beauty and been captured by His love.

Common Misunderstandings

▼
"The Latter Rain Is Just More Power with the Same Message"

This misses the point that the latter rain requires the right message—Christ's righteousness—not merely intensified preaching of law and prophecy. Power comes when the gospel is central.

"The Delay Is Only About World Events"

While global conditions matter, Ellen White taught that the church's unreadiness—specifically resistance to the 1888 message—has prolonged the wait, making spiritual factors primary.

"Character Perfection Comes by Striving Harder"

The 1888 message corrects this by showing that Christlikeness grows through faith and abiding, not through intensified self-effort or fear-based religion.

"Adventist Distinctives Compete with the Gospel"

The truth is that Sabbath, sanctuary, and prophecy find their meaning within the gospel, expressing Christ's lordship rather than standing as tests divorced from grace.

"We Can Hasten the Coming by Better Obedience"

While readiness matters, hastening comes through receiving Christ by faith and allowing the Spirit to work, not through anxious attempts to perfect ourselves independently of grace.

Glossary

▼
Latter Rain

The final outpouring of the Holy Spirit before Christ's return, typified by the agricultural rain in Palestine that ripens the grain for harvest; spiritually, it empowers believers to complete the gospel work and mature in Christlikeness.

Loud Cry

The proclamation of the three angels' messages with great power in the last days, illuminating the earth with knowledge of God's character and calling people to worship the Creator and reject Babylon's deceptions.

Ripeness of the Harvest

The maturity of believers' characters and the completion of gospel witness that signals the world is ready for Christ's return, as grain ripened under the latter rain is ready for reaping.

Glory of Revelation 18

The manifestation of God's character through His people, revealed supremely in the gospel of Christ's righteousness, which fills the earth with light and calls the final generation out of Babylon.

Third Angel's Message in Verity

The third angel's message proclaimed in its true, essential form—centered on Christ's righteousness and faith rather than reduced to warnings about compliance or prophetic symbols.

Conditional Timing

The biblical principle that while God's purposes are certain, the timing of their fulfillment can be affected by human response—readiness hastens, resistance delays.

Early Rain

The outpouring of the Spirit at Pentecost and the apostolic era, which planted the gospel seed and began the church; it prepares for the latter rain that will ripen and finish the work.

Character Ripening

The process of spiritual maturity where faith in Christ produces love, love produces obedience, and obedience reveals Christlikeness—preparing believers to stand in God's presence and reflect His character to the world.
