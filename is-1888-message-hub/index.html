The 1888 Message

Righteousness by faith, the latter rain, and God's final work

The 1888 Minneapolis General Conference marked a pivotal moment in Seventh-day Adventist history. The message of righteousness by faith in Christ alone—championed by Waggoner, Jones, and Ellen White—was intended to re-center Adventism on the gospel and prepare a people for the latter rain and loud cry. Explore the theological content, Ellen White's prophetic endorsement, and the connection to the final work below.

The Content of the Message

The theological core of 1888: justification by faith in Christ alone, the law's proper role as revealer of sin, obedience as fruit not basis, and Christ's finished work as the only ground of acceptance.
