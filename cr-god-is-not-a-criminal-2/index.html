When Power Turns Serpent

*The same energy that sustains life can destroy when severed from love.*

When Pharaoh hardened his heart, the powers of Egypt turned against themselves. The Nile, once life-giving, became a symbol of death. Each plague exposed what happens when creation rejects its Center. The serpent power is not new force from God’s anger but old force freed from His restraint.

“Rods and Serpents” teaches that power, detached from divine motive, reverses polarity. The creative becomes destructive. It is not God changing character but creatures changing relationship. As the connection loosens, the blessing collapses into curse.

Seen this way, divine judgment is not mechanical punishment but moral physics. Sin is entropy of the soul — the slow unravelling of life when it insists on self-direction. God’s “plagues” simply reveal what separation looks like when fully manifest.

Wherever power leaves God’s hand, it becomes perilous. Whether natural forces, human governments, or religious authority, the serpent appears when love no longer governs. That insight prepares us to see why God Himself can never act as a criminal — He never wields power that way.

Sources: Exodus 7–10; Behold Your God, “Rods and Serpents.”
