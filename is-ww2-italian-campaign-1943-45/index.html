Italian Campaign (1943‚Äì45)

From Sicily to the Po Valley: Breaking the Axis in Southern Europe

Core Summary

Beginning with the Allied invasion of Sicily in July 1943 and continuing up the mountainous Italian peninsula until spring 1945, the Italian Campaign tied down large German forces, toppled Mussolini, and opened a vital front in Europe. Despite rugged terrain, fortified defensive lines, and severe weather, Allied armies fought through Salerno, Anzio, and the Gustav and Gothic Lines. Rome fell in June 1944, and the final 1945 offensive shattered German defenses in the Po Valley, contributing to the overall collapse of the Third Reich.
