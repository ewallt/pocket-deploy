Love That Never Fails

God’s love persists through rebellion, misunderstanding, and time.

Maxwell closes his book by tracing the persistence of divine love. From Eden to the cross, God never abandoned His children—even when spurned. Love’s endurance is not weakness but strength: the power to absorb injury without retaliation. This love cannot be exhausted because it flows from the very nature of God Himself.

In contrast to human love, which tires and conditions itself on response, divine love remains constant. It seeks relationship even when rejected. The story of Hosea becomes an emblem of God’s pursuit of estranged hearts.

For Maxwell, salvation is the restoration of confidence in such love. Once we believe that God will never stop loving, fear loses all hold. The universe can rest because it knows its Ruler cannot change.

This chapter insists that divine omnipotence is best understood as omnipotent love—the capacity to keep loving until love wins. Coercion would end freedom; persuasion through sacrifice secures it forever.

“Love never fails” is not sentiment but cosmic law. Everything safe, sane, and eternal depends on that truth.

Sources: *Servants or Friends?*, Ch. 11 “Love That Never Fails.”
