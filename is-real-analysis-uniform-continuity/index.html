Continuous functions on compact sets are uniformly continuous

Concept Snapshot

üß† Historical Note

‚ñº
Origins. Eduard Heine (1870s) formalized the notion of uniform continuity and proved that continuous functions on closed intervals are uniformly continuous. Georg Cantor extended the ideas to general metric spaces.
Foundational Role. The distinction between pointwise and uniform continuity was critical in rigorizing calculus and addressing pathologies like Dirichlet's function (nowhere continuous) and functions continuous at only one point.
