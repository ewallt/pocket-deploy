How Many Reasons Do You Have Not to Love People?

Jesus dismantles every “good reason” we have for withholding love.

Big Idea (Snapshot)

▼

We accumulate reasons—sometimes justified, sometimes protective, sometimes resentful—why certain people do not deserve our love. Jesus never argues that others have earned our love; instead, He reveals the Father who loves us while we are still sinners (Romans 5:8). He calls us to participate in that same love. The question is not whether people qualify, but whether we are willing to let God’s love flow through us freely.

Key Texts

▼

Matthew 5:43–48 — Love your enemies that you may be children of your Father.

Luke 6:27–36 — Do good expecting nothing in return.

Romans 5:8 — God’s love meets us while we are still sinners.

What’s Gone Wrong (Distortion)

▼

We often act as if love is something we dispense based on the other person’s merit. But this assumes we are judges, not witnesses of grace. Jesus exposes this distortion by revealing a Father who “is kind unto the unthankful and to the evil” (Luke 6:35).

What Jesus Shows Us

▼

Jesus reveals a God whose love is courageous, not naïve—self-giving, not conditional. At the cross, He faces hostility without closing His heart, praying, “Father, forgive them” (Luke 23:34). He invites us to give what we have freely received: undeserved love that heals and restores.

Heart Work & Reflection

▼

Who have you quietly decided is too difficult or too wrong to love? What would it mean to release your “good reasons” to Jesus? Where is love asking you to take a small but courageous step?

Gospel Thread

▼

We love because He first loved us (1 John 4:19). When our reasons collapse under the weight of Jesus’ compassion, the gospel becomes not just a belief but a power that transforms how we treat people every day.
