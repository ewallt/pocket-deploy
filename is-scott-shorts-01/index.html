How Many Reasons Do You Have Not to Love People?

Jesus dismantles every “good reason” we have for withholding love.

Big Idea (Snapshot)

▼

We all collect reasons—sometimes excellent, sometimes painful, sometimes justified—why certain people do not deserve our love. Jesus meets these internal defenses head-on. He does not argue that others have earned our love; He reveals that God loves us while we are still enemies, and calls us to participate in that same love. The issue is not whether people qualify, but whether we are willing to let God’s love flow through us freely.

Key Texts

▼

Matthew 5:43–48 — Love your enemies that you may be children of your Father.

Luke 6:27–36 — Do good expecting nothing in return.

Romans 5:8 — While we were still sinners, Christ died for us.

What’s Gone Wrong (Distortion)

▼

We often place ourselves in the judge’s seat and determine who is worthy of our love. We may feel morally superior, self-protective, or simply exhausted. But all these rationalizations reveal an underlying belief: love is something we dispense based on the other person’s merit. Jesus exposes this as a distortion. Love that evaluates worthiness is not the love of the Father.

What Jesus Shows Us

▼

Jesus reveals a God who loves enemies without hesitation. He demonstrates a love that absorbs rejection, misunderstanding, hostility, and betrayal—yet never closes the door. His love is not naive; it is courageous and truthful. He calls us to give what we have freely received: undeserved love that heals and restores.

Heart Work & Reflection

▼

Who have you quietly decided is too difficult, too hurtful, or too wrong to love? What would it mean to release your “good reasons” to Jesus? Where is love asking you to take a small, courageous step?

Gospel Thread

▼

We love because He first loved us. When our reasons collapse under the weight of Jesus’ compassion, the gospel becomes more than a belief—it becomes the power that transforms how we treat people every day.
