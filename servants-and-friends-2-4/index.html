Judgment as Healing

God’s judgment exposes reality so that restoration becomes possible.

Maxwell redefines divine judgment as an act of diagnosis rather than condemnation. God judges by revealing truth—showing each heart what it has chosen. In this light, judgment is not the end of mercy but its completion.

Throughout the Bible, judgment scenes have redemptive aims. David, Isaiah, and Peter all faced painful exposure that led to renewal. The final judgment will serve the same purpose for the universe: to establish eternal trust in God’s justice.

The Great White Throne is not a courtroom of terror but a cosmic unveiling. Every question will be answered, every doubt resolved. Transparency is God’s method of vindication.

When truth is fully known, rebellion will collapse under its own falsity. Healing comes through honesty. Judgment restores order because it removes deception, not because it imposes fear.

To welcome judgment is to welcome light. Friends of God rejoice that nothing remains hidden between them and the One they trust completely.

Sources: *Servants or Friends?*, Ch. 9 “Judgment as Healing.”
