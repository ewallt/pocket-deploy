Iwo Jima (1945)

Securing Airfields and Iconic Sacrifice in the Pacific

Core Summary

From February 19 to March 26, 1945, U.S. Marines fought one of the bloodiest battles of the Pacific War to capture the volcanic island of Iwo Jima. The eight square-mile island held strategic airfields needed for B-29 bomber operations against Japan. Fierce Japanese resistance from fortified positions led to over 26,000 American casualties, while nearly all 21,000 Japanese defenders fought to the death. The iconic flag-raising on Mount Suribachi became one of the war's most enduring images.
