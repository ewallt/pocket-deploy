Osborne Reef (Florida)

South Florida wanted an artificial reef to boost marine habitat and tourism.

Sink ~2 million discarded car tires offshore—cheap, durable, easy to stack.

Storms snapped the ties; tires roamed the seafloor, sandblasting natural reefs and entangling wildlife. Cleanup divers are still chasing the “permanent” fix.

Rent Control

Keep housing affordable for tenants in expensive cities.

Cap allowable rent increases and limit evictions to protect existing renters.

Landlords convert units to condos or withdraw them from rental markets entirely; new construction slows; maintenance declines. The lucky few keep cheap flats—everyone else finds fewer flats to rent at all.

Minimum Wage Increases

Tariffs and Trade Wars

Impose tariffs and quotas on imported goods to raise their prices and level the playing field.

Cap prices or forbid “excessive” increases after disasters.

Zoning and Height Limits

Fuel Economy Standards (Rebound & Mix)

Cut gasoline use and tailpipe emissions.

Eviction Moratoria

Victims (e.g., domestic violence survivors) can be labeled the nuisance and evicted, discouraging future calls for help and making neighborhoods less safe.

Jaywalking Laws

Reduce pedestrian crashes.

Ticket crossings outside marked crosswalks; raise fines to deter risky behavior.

Criminalizes normal responses to car-centric street design; imposes high fines on people who walk and can even lead to extreme prosecutions when safe crossings are impractically far away.

Fines for Minor Infractions

Encourage compliance with local rules (traffic, parking, small code violations).

Use fines, fees, and court costs; suspend licenses or escalate penalties for nonpayment.

Costs snowball into debt, license loss, job loss, and even jail—effectively penalizing poverty rather than deterring harm.

Privatizing Water

Transfer operation or ownership to private utilities.

Higher rates, less transparency, and poorer service—especially in rural or low-income communities—leaving residents paying more for a basic necessity.

Raises construction costs, kills small infill, spreads everything farther apart—so people drive more to reach anything. The cure for parking scarcity creates…more driving and parking scarcity.

South Asia’s Vulture Collapse (Diclofenac Ban Saga)

Farmers in South Asia needed affordable pain relief for working livestock; keeping cattle healthy protects household income and food security.

Widespread veterinary use of diclofenac: effective, cheap, and great for sore bovines—so routine that residues were common in carcasses left for scavengers.

Vultures ate those carcasses, suffered diclofenac-induced kidney failure, and crashed by ~95% in some species. With vultures gone, dog populations swelled around rotting carrion, driving more rabies risk; governments later banned veterinary diclofenac and promoted safer substitutes like meloxicam.

Kingpin Decapitation Strategy

Drug-cartel violence in Latin America, with governments under pressure to show decisive progress.

“Decapitation” strategies: target kingpins for arrest or neutralisation, plus high-visibility crackdowns to disrupt trafficking routes. Looks tough, polls great.

Remove a boss and you invite splintering and turf wars. Evidence from Mexico links kingpin targeting to cartel fragmentation and sharply higher homicide rates—cut off the head, grow three.

School Cash Transfers (Morocco)

Morocco wanted to cut primary-school dropout and boost enrolment.

Scale up a conditional cash transfer (Tayssir), paying families if children enrol and attend. It worked: dropout fell by about 1.3 percentage points.

Supply didn’t scale with demand. Average class size rose by 3.6 students and test scores fell. Schooling went up; learning went down—at least until resources caught up.

Biometric ID for Welfare Delivery (India’s Aadhaar)

Fraud and “ghost” beneficiaries plagued welfare programs; the fix seemed obvious: tighten verification, digitize payments, and cut leakages.

Link benefits to a national biometric ID (fingerprints/iris scans) to authenticate and automate disbursements directly.

Digital precision met analog realities: fingerprint mismatches, power cuts, and poor connectivity sometimes locked out legitimate recipients. Efficiency rose on paper; exclusion rose on the ground.

Sin Taxes and Substitution Effects

Governments sought to curb unhealthy consumption—sugar, alcohol, tobacco—through excise taxes that would nudge better habits.

Levy targeted “sin taxes” to make vice pricier and virtue more appealing; revenue doubles as a public-health win.

Consumers substitute: sugary-drink taxes can shift purchases to untaxed high-calorie items; smokers trade down to cheaper brands or informal markets. One vice down, several side quests unlocked.

Housing Subsidies and Urban Sprawl (Indonesia’s Mortgage Incentives)

Urban housing shortages made affordable ownership politically urgent.

Offer mortgage interest subsidies and credit programs for low-income buyers to expand the formal housing market.

Most take-up occurred on the distant fringe where land is cheap but infrastructure thin. Commutes lengthened, emissions rose, and informal settlements persisted near jobs. Units up, urban efficiency down.
