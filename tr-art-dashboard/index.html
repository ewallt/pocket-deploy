Masters of Modern Art

Visual exploration of pioneering artists from Impressionism through Post-Impressionism, examining the revolutionary techniques and visions that transformed art from the late 19th into the early 20th century.

Modern Art Chronicles

Comprehensive journey through the evolution of modern art, tracing the progression from Impressionist innovations through Post-Impressionist experiments to the birth of Modernism and its diverse movements.

Art of the Gilded Age

Exploration of American art during the Gilded Age (1870s-1900), examining how rapid industrialization, extreme wealth disparity, and social transformation influenced artistic expression and patronage.

The Hudson River School

Study of America's first major art movement, exploring how 19th-century landscape painters captured the sublime beauty of the American wilderness and shaped national identity through romantic depictions of nature.

The Barbizon School

Investigation of the French artistic movement that pioneered plein air painting and naturalistic landscapes, laying crucial groundwork for Impressionism through their direct observation of rural life and nature.
