Siege of Leningrad (1941‚Äì44)

872 Days of Encirclement, Starvation, and Endurance

Core Summary

From September 1941 to January 1944, German and Finnish forces besieged Leningrad (now St. Petersburg) in one of the longest and most devastating sieges in history. Over a million civilians perished, primarily from starvation and cold. Despite immense suffering, the city never surrendered, and Soviet forces eventually broke the blockade in 1944, transforming the siege into a symbol of resilience and sacrifice.
