The Security of the Universe

Eternal safety rests on trust, not control.

Maxwell shows that the universe’s permanence depends on faith in God’s character. Force can compel obedience but never loyalty. Only truth and love can create a community incapable of rebellion because it has seen what rebellion costs.

After the cross, every intelligent being has evidence enough to trust God forever. The spectacle of Calvary was not for appeasement but for revelation—the unmasking of lies that began in heaven. With deception gone, harmony becomes voluntary and unbreakable.

God’s government therefore needs no prisons or threats. Freedom is preserved because everyone freely agrees that love’s way is best. The security of the universe is moral, not mechanical.

In that future order, divine law will be understood as description, not demand—the natural expression of life lived in trust. Judgment and fear will vanish; joy and cooperation will take their place.

Power has failed to secure peace; only love has succeeded. That truth ensures that rebellion will never rise again.

Sources: *Servants or Friends?*, Ch. 12 “The Security of the Universe.”
