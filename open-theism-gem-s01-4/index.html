Open Theism: Introduction and Core Tenets

Part 4 · Divine Providence in an Open Future: How God Guides and Responds

A common objection to Open Theism is that it weakens or even negates the concept of divine providence—the belief that God sovereignly governs and directs the world to fulfill His purposes. If the future is not exhaustively known by God, how can He be in control? How can believers trust that His ultimate will shall be done? Open Theists respond by offering a different model of providence, one that emphasizes God's infinite wisdom and resourcefulness over meticulous control.

In the Open View, divine providence is not about controlling every detail according to a pre-written script. Instead, it is about God's masterful ability to guide, influence, and respond to the unfolding actions of free creatures to achieve His ultimate goals. God is like a grandmaster of chess who, despite not knowing the opponent's exact moves in advance, has the wisdom and skill to respond to any possible move and steer the game toward the desired outcome. God's sovereignty is not compromised; it is expressed dynamically and relationally.

This model of providence highlights several key aspects of God's character. First is God's infinite intelligence. While God may not know the future choices of free agents, He knows all the possibilities and can prepare for every contingency. His plans are not brittle; they are flexible and robust, capable of incorporating human decisions without being thwarted by them. God sets the ultimate destination, but the exact path may involve collaboration and response to human choices.

Second is God's persuasive love. Rather than coercing outcomes, God works through invitation, persuasion, and inspiration. He calls people to partner with Him in His work, respecting their freedom to accept or reject that call. The biblical narrative is filled with examples of God empowering prophets, leaders, and ordinary people to carry out His will. This collaborative model makes human actions genuinely significant in the accomplishment of God's purposes.

Finally, God's providence involves His unwavering faithfulness to His promises. While specific paths may change, God's ultimate promises—such as the final redemption of creation and the triumph of good over evil—are non-negotiable. Open Theists affirm that God has the power and wisdom to ensure these ultimate goals are met, regardless of human choices. He can work around human failure and rebellion, bringing good out of unintended consequences. Therefore, trust in God is not trust in a detailed blueprint, but trust in a supremely capable and loving Person who has promised to see His creation through to a good end.
