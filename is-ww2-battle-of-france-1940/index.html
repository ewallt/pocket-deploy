Battle of France (1940)

Blitzkrieg in the West: The Fall of France and the Low Countries

Core Summary

From May to June 1940, Germany defeated France, Belgium, and the Netherlands through a rapid, combined-arms offensive that bypassed the Maginot Line via the Ardennes. Panzer spearheads broke through at Sedan and raced to the Channel, encircling Allied armies in northern France and Belgium. After the evacuation at Dunkirk, a second phase overran the remaining French defenses, leading to the armistice on 22 June and a strategic transformation of the war in Europe.
