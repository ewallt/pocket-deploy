All Things New

The universe at peace because everyone freely trusts the God who is love.

Atonement ends in renewal: a world where fear is forgotten and friendship is the atmosphere of life. Maxwell’s quotations look forward to that day not as reward but as reality at last—truth loved, lies gone, and joy unafraid.

Newness begins now wherever God is known as He truly is. Hearts rest, consciences clear, and communities heal as trust replaces suspicion. The future simply completes what starts here.

In the renewed creation, law is no longer argued; it is admired as the pattern of love. Worship is discovery, and discovery is delight.

The security of the universe is moral, not mechanical. It lasts because every mind agrees with what love has shown. Rebellion has no audience left.

All things new means relationships new—God with us, us with one another, and all with the truth. That is the fruit of atonement’s long persuasion.

Friends of God will call it home forever.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations, entries on “New Earth,” “Peace,” “Trust.”
