God Is Exactly Like Jesus

The most important truth about God is that He looks like Christ — always.

Maxwell’s favorite affirmation — “God is not someone to be afraid of” — rests on this claim: the Father is exactly like the Son. Jesus is not the kinder face of a sterner God; He is “the exact representation of His being.”

At the cross, the humility, patience, and non-retaliating love of Jesus unveil the Father’s heart. If we have seen Christ forgive His enemies and bear their hatred, we have seen how God treats His enemies.

This conviction reorients doctrine. Law, judgment, wrath, and justice must be understood in a way consistent with Jesus’ character or they are being misunderstood. Any theology that makes God unlike Christ must be revised.

Seeing God in Jesus also restores prayer. We speak to a Friend who understands our weakness because He has shared it. Confidence replaces dread; honesty becomes possible.

When this vision prevails, worship changes tone. Gratitude grows where fear withers. The gospel’s power is the beauty of the One it reveals.

Atonement achieves its goal when this truth is believed: the Almighty has always been like Jesus; we have not always known it. Faith is simply recognizing His face.
