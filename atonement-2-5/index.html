Freedom Secured

When truth has won the heart, freedom is safe forever.

Freedom was the risk of creation and the goal of redemption. God did not design automatons but beings capable of love. Maxwell emphasized that atonement secures freedom by making truth and trust unbreakable partners.

When the cross revealed God’s character, it ended the need for coercion. Every mind that saw it understood that God’s way alone brings life. The universe will never again doubt Him because He has been fully known.

This is what Maxwell called the “eternal security of the universe.” It rests not on law enforcement but on love’s persuasiveness. Peace is guaranteed by truth believed, not fear imposed.

Freedom secured means creatures remain free to rebel but no longer wish to. The risk continues; the desire does not. Love has won its case.

At the cross, God’s power and freedom were shown to be identical in purpose: both exist to protect life through truth. That is why His government will never fail.

In the end, freedom is not what we gain from God but what we share with Him — the liberty of truth loved without fear.

Sources: Graham Maxwell, Atonement: Quotations (Pine Knoll PDF), entries on “Freedom,” “Security of the Universe,” “Truth.”
