# Open Theism Codex Wiki

This wiki curates the Open Theism Codex articles into a navigable reference. Each entry distills the library's seven-paragraph studies into a format that supports quick lookup, cross-referencing, and further exploration.

## Library Structure

### Series 1 — Introduction and Core Tenets

1. [What is Open Theism?](./open-theism-codex-s01-1.md) — Defines the movement's core convictions about divine knowledge, creaturely freedom, and relational sovereignty.
2. [God, Time, and Foreknowledge](./open-theism-codex-s01-2.md) — Examines how open theists reinterpret omniscience, temporality, and the ontology of future contingents.
3. [The Argument from Human Freedom](./open-theism-codex-s01-3.md) — Evaluates claims that exhaustive foreknowledge undermines reciprocal love and genuine choice.
4. [Divine Providence in an Open Future](./open-theism-codex-s01-4.md) — Explores responsive providence, pastoral implications, and adaptive guidance.

### Series 2 — Scriptural Basis, History, and Key Debates
1. [Interpreting Predictive Prophecy](./open-theism-codex-s02-1.md) — Outlines conditional prophecy and the distinction between divine intention and exhaustive scripting.
2. [Key Old Testament Texts](./open-theism-codex-s02-2.md) — Investigates passages such as Genesis 6, Exodus 32, and Jonah 3 with an openness hermeneutic.
3. [Key New Testament Texts](./open-theism-codex-s02-3.md) — Analyzes themes of foreknowledge, prayer, and agency across the Gospels and Epistles.
4. [Historical Roots and Precedents](./open-theism-codex-s02-4.md) — Traces antecedents from patristic debates to modern evangelical discussions.
5. [Open Theism vs. Classical Theism](./open-theism-codex-s02-5.md) — Compares divine attributes, metaphysics, and hermeneutical commitments.

### Series 3 — Criticisms, Implications, and Contemporary Relevance
1. [Criticisms of Open Theism](./open-theism-codex-s03-1.md) — Summarizes objections about sovereignty, prophecy, and doctrinal coherence.
2. [Alternative Models](./open-theism-codex-s03-2.md) — Contrasts Arminianism, Molinism, and Calvinism as different accounts of foreknowledge and freedom.
3. [Practical Implications](./open-theism-codex-s03-3.md) — Reflects on prayer, pastoral care, and faithful living amid uncertainty.
4. [Philosophical Considerations](./open-theism-codex-s03-4.md) — Engages analytic discussions about time, modality, and logic.
5. [Open Theism Today](./open-theism-codex-s03-5.md) — Reviews current scholarship, denominational responses, and future research paths.
