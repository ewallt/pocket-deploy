The Resurrection and the Universe

*Power confirms truth—the Father vindicates the crucified Son.*

If the cross reveals God’s heart, the resurrection confirms His rule. Love is not merely noble; it is effective. The Father’s raising of Jesus is heaven’s public endorsement of the Crucified, declaring that sacrificial love is the law of life and therefore indestructible.

Resurrection reframes power. It is not the reversal of Calvary but its consequence. The same patience that refused coercion now overcomes death without becoming like the forces it defeats. Power serves revelation: it validates what the cross said about God.

For the universe, this means hope is realistic. Reality is finally aligned with the character of the Lamb; history is moving toward the recognition of His worthiness. The scars on a living Lord guarantee that justice will be both truthful and tender.

For disciples, resurrection power is not license to dominate but capacity to endure and to serve. The church practices strength under the sign of the empty tomb: courage without cruelty, holiness without haughtiness, mission without manipulation.

Thus the risen Christ stands as the world’s true future—truth that cannot stay buried, love that cannot stay silenced, and life that cannot stay confined.

Sources: Romans 1:4; Acts 2:24–36; Revelation 5:6; Behold Your God (resurrection as vindication).
