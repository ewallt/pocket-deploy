ğŸ§  Historical Note

â–¼
Origins. Isaac Newton (1660s) and Gottfried Leibniz (1670s) independently discovered the FTC, though neither proved it rigorously. Newton viewed it as relating "fluxions" (derivatives) to "fluents" (antiderivatives).
Rigorization. Augustin-Louis Cauchy (1820s) gave the first rigorous definition of the integral and proof of the FTC using limits. Bernhard Riemann (1850s) refined the integral definition.
