Okinawa (1945)

The Bloodiest Battle of the Pacific and the Final Major Campaign

Core Summary

From April 1 to June 22, 1945, Allied forces fought the largest amphibious assault of the Pacific War to capture Okinawa, a strategic island only 340 miles from mainland Japan. The 82-day battle resulted in over 100,000 Japanese military deaths, 12,000 American deaths, and an estimated 100,000 Okinawan civilian casualties. Massive kamikaze attacks inflicted unprecedented naval losses. The horrific cost of Okinawa strongly influenced the decision to use atomic weapons rather than invade Japan.
