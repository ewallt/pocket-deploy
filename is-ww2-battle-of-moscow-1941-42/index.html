Battle of Moscow (1941‚Äì42)

The First Major German Defeat on Land and the Halt of Operation Barbarossa

Core Summary

From October 1941 to January 1942, German Army Group Centre advanced toward the Soviet capital in Operation Typhoon but was stopped at the gates of Moscow by stubborn defenses, autumn mud, and the onset of severe winter. Reinforced by Siberian divisions and coordinated counterattacks, the Red Army launched a December offensive that pushed the Wehrmacht back from the city. The failure to seize Moscow shattered the myth of German invincibility and ended hopes of a quick victory in the East.
