Open Theism: Criticisms, Implications, and Contemporary Relevance

Part 4 · Philosophical Considerations: Logic, Time, and Possibility

Content paragraph 1 for Philosophical Considerations. This explores the philosophical foundations of Open Theism, particularly its views on the nature of time and temporal reality.

Content paragraph 2 for Philosophical Considerations. Further analysis of whether Open Theism requires an A-theory (tensed) or can accommodate a B-theory (tenseless) view of time.

Content paragraph 3 for Philosophical Considerations. Discussion of modal logic and how Open Theism understands possibility, actuality, and the ontological status of future contingents.

Content paragraph 4 for Philosophical Considerations. Examination of the principle of bivalence and whether future contingent propositions have truth values in Open Theist metaphysics.

Content paragraph 5 for Philosophical Considerations. Comparison with process theology and other philosophical theologies that challenge classical divine attributes.

Content paragraph 6 for Philosophical Considerations. Analysis of the relationship between divine omniscience and the scope of knowable reality in Open Theist thought.

Content paragraph 7 for Philosophical Considerations. Concluding thoughts on the philosophical coherence of Open Theism and ongoing debates in analytic philosophy of religion.
