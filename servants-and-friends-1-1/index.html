Would You Be Afraid to Meet God?

When we see Him as He really is, fear melts into awe and trust.

Many people have learned to fear God because religion has painted Him in terrifying colors. Graham Maxwell opens his book by asking whether we would enjoy meeting God face-to-face today. Our answer, he says, reveals what we believe about His character. If He is arbitrary or vindictive, fear is rational; but if He is exactly like Jesus, then trust is the only reasonable response.

Through Scripture, God has been misrepresented as cruel, vengeful, and unpredictable. Maxwell argues that these pictures reflect human projection rather than divine reality. The cross stands as God’s self-revelation, disproving Satan’s charges and showing that love alone governs the universe.

When Moses trembled at Sinai, God’s answer was not more thunder but gentle assurance: “I am with you.” Throughout history, He has tried to replace fear with faith by demonstrating His trustworthiness in relationship after relationship.

Fear remains because we don’t really know Him. Knowledge of God and trust in God grow together — and that knowledge comes through the life and death of Christ. The more clearly we see Jesus, the less we dread the Father.

The goal of the gospel is not to appease God but to heal our mistrust. When love casts out fear, we are ready to meet Him as friends.
