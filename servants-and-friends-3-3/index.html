The End of Fear

When God’s character is fully known, fear has no reason to exist.

Fear is the last remnant of misunderstanding in the universe. Maxwell shows that God’s plan culminates in its removal. The final judgment does not frighten the redeemed; it comforts them. When truth about God shines without distortion, fear becomes impossible.

Every act of revelation—from Sinai to Calvary—has aimed to replace fear with trust. What began in Eden as doubt will end in certainty born of experience. The fire of God’s presence will consume lies, not people.

The wicked perish because they cannot endure reality, not because God attacks them. They flee from love too pure for deceit to survive. In contrast, the friends of God rejoice in the same light that others resist.

Perfect love casts out fear because it removes its cause—misunderstanding. The gospel is not the story of how sinners escape punishment, but of how fear is driven from the heart of creation.

When fear is gone, worship becomes joy, and the universe sings in unshadowed freedom.

Sources: *Servants or Friends?*, Ch. 13 “The End of Fear.”
